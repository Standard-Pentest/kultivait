LLM routing · context hygiene

The greenest token is the one you never send.

Kultivait sits between your tools and your models. Every prompt is weighed by a local embedding, routed to the cheapest model that can carry it — your own garden first, the cloud only when it earns its cost — and every phase handoff is pruned before it grows wild.

Most frontier calls are weeds. In our own agentic sessions, roughly two of every three prompts sent to a frontier model — renames, formatting, summaries — were work an 8B model handles cleanly.

Context compounds. A 50-turn coding session re-reads its own stale context on every turn. You pay the multiplier, the datacenter burns it, and the model wades through it.

Nobody is watching. Your tools pick a model once and never reconsider. The decision that costs you the most is the one nothing in your stack is making.

The pipeline

How it grows

Five stages, in order. Each one runs on your machine, and the decision to spend money is never made by spending money.

01

Intercept

Point your tools at Kultivait instead of the API — one OpenAI-compatible endpoint. Your editor, your agents, your scripts: nothing else changes.

02

Weigh

A 274 MB local embedding model — nomic-embed-text — reads the prompt in milliseconds and scores its true difficulty. No cloud call decides whether to make a cloud call.

03

Route

Formatting and renames go to llama3.1:8b. Local reasoning to qwen3:14b. Cross-file architecture to claude. Doc-grounded checks to gemini via agy.

04

Prune

At every phase gate — explore, plan, build, verify — a local model distills the context to what the next phase actually needs. The rest is composted.

05

Harvest

Every routed token and pruned kilobyte is tallied: dollars kept in your pocket, watt-hours never drawn, context kept clean enough to trust.

Context hygiene

Pruning is not deletion. It's how gardens stay alive.

Routing saves you the difference between models. Pruning saves you the multiplier on every call. At each phase gate, Kultivait distills what the session learned into a brief the next phase can trust — and composts the rest.

gate 1explore → plan
carried in 96K tokhanded off 1.8K

Forty files read, three dead ends walked. The plan phase receives the findings — not the walk.

gate 2plan → build
carried in 41K tokhanded off 1.2K

Debated alternatives stay behind. The build phase gets the decision, the constraints, and the file list.

gate 3build → verify
carried in 74K tokhanded off 2.4K

Verification starts fresh with the diff and the claims to check — clean enough to actually catch mistakes.

A gardener prunes so the plant thrives — not to make it smaller. Distillation runs on your local models, so hygiene itself costs nothing.

Ethos

Three principles, in order

Kultivait is built on a hierarchy, not a slogan. The order matters.

i.

Reduce

The token you never generate costs nothing and burns nothing — no caveats, no asterisks. Context hygiene comes first because reduction is the only saving that is pure.

ii.

Right-size

An 8-billion-parameter model answering a formatting question instead of a frontier model is a real, order-of-magnitude reduction in compute — wherever that compute runs.

iii.

Localize

Work that fits your machine stays on your machine: free, private, and off the grid of metered inference. The cloud remains in the rotation — for the work that deserves it.

An honest note on the math. We won't tell you your laptop beats a batched datacenter on every watt — sometimes it doesn't. We count what we can measure: tokens never sent, compute right-sized, and dollars that stayed in your pocket. Cultivation is a practice, not a purity test.
